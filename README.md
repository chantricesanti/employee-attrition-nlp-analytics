# Employee Attrition Insights Using NLP

This project applies natural language processing and people analytics to employee attrition interview responses. The goal was to transform open-ended employee feedback into structured retention insights that could help stakeholders understand recurring workforce concerns, reasons for leaving, and opportunities to improve employee experience.

## Project Overview

Employee attrition data often contains valuable qualitative feedback, but open-ended survey and exit-interview responses can be difficult to analyze consistently at scale. This project uses NLP techniques to clean, structure, and analyze employee feedback across categories such as reasons for leaving, lowlights, highlights, suggestions, and return considerations.

The analysis surfaces recurring retention themes and translates unstructured responses into stakeholder-ready findings for workforce and employee experience discussions.

## Scope

- Analyzed employee attrition interview and survey responses across multiple feedback categories.
- Cleaned and structured open-ended text responses for NLP-based people analytics.
- Applied tokenization, lemmatization, n-gram analysis, TF-IDF, and topic modeling to identify recurring themes.
- Compared patterns across feedback categories to distinguish repeated workforce concerns from isolated comments.
- Translated unstructured employee feedback into insights on attrition drivers, employee experience gaps, and retention opportunities.

## Methods

The workflow included:

1. Text cleaning and preprocessing  
2. Tokenization and lemmatization  
3. N-gram analysis  
4. TF-IDF feature extraction  
5. Topic modeling and recurring theme identification  
6. Interpretation of results for stakeholder reporting  

## Key Findings

The analysis showed that attrition was not driven by one isolated concern, but by repeated patterns across employee feedback. Themes around workload pressure, career growth, management support, compensation, and workplace experience appeared across reasons for leaving, lowlights, and suggestions, indicating that retention concerns were connected to both day-to-day employee experience and longer-term career expectations.

The project also showed the value of analyzing open-ended feedback systematically. By grouping recurring themes across exit responses and return considerations, the analysis helped distinguish broader workforce concerns from individual comments and made the findings easier to communicate to stakeholders.
